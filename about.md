# About Evan Foster

I live and work in Springfield, Illinois. I am a researcher, writer and founder whose work focuses on governance, financialization and the structural conditions that shape how organizations recognize value and make decisions. The path that brought me here was not a straight one.

I grew up in Illinois and left high school during my senior year. As a teenager, I made a series of bad decisions that led to two years in Illinois youth correctional facilities in Murphysboro and Harrisburg. That period forced me to confront consequences early. It also forced me to decide whether my life was going to keep moving in that direction or not.

When I came home, work came first. I spent several years in the automotive industry, starting at the bottom and eventually moving into finance. By my early twenties, I had become a Finance Director at a car dealership, responsible for structuring vehicle financing and working inside the financial side of the business every day.

That experience mattered more than I understood at the time. It gave me an unfiltered view of how incentives shape behavior inside organizations and how people learn to adapt to systems that reward the wrong things. It was also the point where I realized I did not want to spend my life inside a structure that trained people to treat distortion as normal.

I left the automotive industry planning to pursue medicine. Education became the path forward, and I eventually enrolled at the University of Illinois Springfield. I earned a Bachelor of Science in Biology and later a Master of Public Health from the University of Illinois Springfield. As family responsibilities grew, the long road of medical training no longer fit the kind of life I wanted to build. My work shifted toward public health, systems and organizational structure.

That path led me to the American Lung Association, where I served as Senior Director of Healthy Air Solutions. In that role, I led national environmental health initiatives, worked across multiple states and managed multimillion-dollar program portfolios.

Working inside large organizations exposed the same pattern over and over. Decisions were usually not limited by effort or good intentions. They were limited by structure. Financial accounting had formal authority. Other forms of value that organizations clearly depended on such as operational capacity, learning, long-term resilience and external impact usually did not. Those forms of value still mattered, but they were easier to delay, harder to defend and more likely to be translated back into financial terms before they could carry weight.

That pattern is what led me to develop Integrated Value Architecture. It is a governance framework designed to restore structural balance between different forms of organizational value. The framework introduces a multi-ledger architecture so financial, operational, capacity, learning and innovation, and externalities and equity domains can each be recognized and governed within their own structures instead of being routed through finance by default.

Alongside that work, I write and conduct research on governance, financialization and organizational structure. I am currently pursuing a Doctor of Public Administration at the University of Illinois Springfield, where my research examines financialization and resource distribution in public institutions.

I was also raised in the LDS Church. I am less active than I once was, but that background still shaped how I think about responsibility, community and institutional life.

Some people who search my name will also find two Springfield Herald News police blotter pages. Those pages reproduce daily arrest logs. In both cases involving me, no charges were filed and the matters did not proceed further. I do not pretend those pages do not exist. They do. They are public. They are also not an accurate representation of my life, my work or the direction I chose years ago.

My life has been shaped by rebuilding, education, public service and a refusal to stay where bad decisions first landed me. Today I continue to live and work in [Springfield, Illinois](/springfield/) while developing [Integrated Value Architecture](https://integratedvaluearchitecture.com) and pursuing doctoral research at the University of Illinois Springfield.
