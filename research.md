# Research by Evan Foster

I write and conduct research on governance, financialization, public administration and organizational structure. My work focuses on the structural conditions that shape how institutions recognize value, allocate authority and make decisions over time.

I am based in Springfield, Illinois and currently pursuing a Doctor of Public Administration at the University of Illinois Springfield. My doctoral research examines financialization and resource distribution in public institutions.

Alongside my academic work, I am the founder of [Integrated Value Architecture](https://integratedvaluearchitecture.com), a governance framework developed to address the structural imbalance that develops when financial accounting becomes the dominant authority inside organizations.

If you are searching for Evan Foster’s research, this page brings together my current research focus, working paper and external research profiles.

## Current Research Focus

My current research explores how organizations become structurally misaligned when financial measures become the main authoritative ledger for decision-making. When that happens, other forms of value may still be discussed, but they are easier to delay, harder to defend and more likely to be translated into financial terms before they carry weight.

That problem shows up across sectors. Public institutions, nonprofits and private organizations are often expected to protect operational capability, long-term resilience, learning, equity and external impact while still routing decisions through one dominant accounting structure. My research examines what that does to resource distribution, institutional behavior and organizational overload.

This work also informs the development of Integrated Value Architecture, a five-ledger governance framework designed to restore structural balance across financial, operational, capacity, learning and innovation, and externalities and equity domains.
