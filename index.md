# Evan Foster

I am a systems builder for organizations, and most of my work starts in the same place. I keep seeing environments where people already know what is wrong, already know where the work gets stuck, and already know who ends up carrying too much of it, but the structure still keeps routing decisions badly enough that the same mess comes back in a slightly different outfit a few weeks later. That has been true in finance, in healthcare and public health settings, in government, in nonprofit work and in board governance. The titles change faster than the pattern does.

This site is the public home for that body of work. Some of it is academic. Some of it is practical. Some of it is writing. Some of it is governance architecture. Some of it is just me trying to say plainly what I have watched happen too many times to keep pretending it is random. That includes my doctoral research, my work through [Integrated Value Architecture](https://integratedvaluearchitecture.com), my board role with [350 Chicago](/350-chicago/), my academic path through [Lincoln Land Community College and the University of Illinois Springfield](/academics/), my book project *Always Over Capacity*, and the research and writing that keep growing out of all of it.

I did not come to this from one clean professional lane. I came to it from seeing what happens when finance becomes the place everything has to pass through, when work keeps getting pushed uphill to people farther from it, and when the people closest to the problem keep getting handed the cleanup on the back end. Sometimes that looks like a budget problem. Sometimes it looks like approvals. Sometimes it looks like staffing, delay, overload or strategy that never really survives contact with the organization that is supposed to carry it. Most of the time, it is the same structural issue showing up in a different room.

## Current Work

My current work runs across several lanes at once because that is how the real thing developed, not because I needed a fancier way to organize a website.

[Integrated Value Architecture](https://integratedvaluearchitecture.com) is the most developed public expression of that work. I built it because I got tired of watching organizations force every serious problem through finance just because finance was the only place with a real ledger, real authority and a durable way to make decisions stick. The point was never to attack finance. The point was to stop making finance carry everything nobody else had been given standing to hold.

My doctoral work at the University of Illinois Springfield studies how federal tax policy reinforces financialization across sectors. That sounds academic because it is, but the reason I care about it came long before the dissertation. I had already spent years watching organizations make the same kinds of choices for the same structural reasons and then act surprised when the consequences landed where they always land.

My writing does the same thing in a more public way. I use it to name what I have seen, where it breaks, who ends up carrying it and why the usual explanations are often too clean to be true.
