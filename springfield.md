# Springfield, Illinois

Springfield, Illinois is where I live and where much of my work is based.

While my professional work has taken me across many states, Springfield has remained the place where my research, writing, and development of Integrated Value Architecture take place. It is also where I am pursuing my Doctor of Public Administration at the University of Illinois Springfield.

Springfield sits at an unusual intersection of public institutions, policy development, and community life. As the capital of Illinois, it provides a close view of how government agencies, nonprofit organizations, and private institutions interact in practice. That environment has influenced much of my thinking about governance architecture and institutional structure.

The city is also where much of my personal and professional life has unfolded over the past decade. My work with the American Lung Association included programs that operated across multiple states, but the analytical and research work behind those efforts was often done from Springfield.

Today my research, writing, and development of Integrated Value Architecture continue to take place here while I pursue doctoral work at the University of Illinois Springfield.

Springfield remains the place where I think, write, and build.
