# Evan Foster

I am a researcher, writer and founder based in Springfield, Illinois. My work focuses on the structural problems that shape how institutions make decisions, distribute authority and bury important work until the consequences get harder to ignore.

This site is the public home for my work across research, writing, governance architecture and independent projects. That includes my doctoral research, Integrated Value Architecture, my book project, public writing and other work I am building over time.

A lot of my work starts from the same basic problem. Organizations are expected to protect service quality, staffing capacity, learning, long-term resilience, community impact and financial performance at the same time, but only some of those things ever get real authority, durable records or a place to stand inside the structure. That gap shapes what gets funded, delayed, defended or quietly dropped. It also shapes how much overload people are expected to absorb before anyone admits the structure is the problem.

I built this site to make that body of work easier to follow in one place.

## Current Work

### Integrated Value Architecture

I am the Founder and Principal Architect of [Integrated Value Architecture](https://integratedvaluearchitecture.com), a five-ledger governance architecture built to address the structural failure that develops when finance becomes the default home for every important decision, unresolved tradeoff and unowned form of value.

### Doctoral Research

I am pursuing a Doctor of Public Administration at the University of Illinois Springfield. My research focuses on governance, financialization and resource distribution in public institutions, especially the point where organizations say certain things matter but never give them real authority inside the system. More is available on the [Research](/research/) page.

### Writing

I write about governance, financialization, structural overload and the gap between what institutions claim to value and what they are actually built to protect. That includes public-facing essays, working papers and longer-form projects in development.

### Always Over Capacity

I am currently developing *Always Over Capacity*, a book about the structural causes of overload, buried value and decision systems that keep routing important work into the wrong places.

### Projects

My work is not limited to research and governance. I am also building independent projects, including apps and other tools, as part of a broader portfolio of work that reflects how I think, what I notice and what I want to build over time.

## Background

Before founding Integrated Value Architecture, I served as Senior Director of Healthy Air Solutions at the American Lung Association, where I led national environmental health initiatives and managed major program and grant portfolios across multiple states. Earlier in my career, I worked in the automotive industry and became a Finance Director at a car dealership in my early twenties. Across very different settings, I kept seeing the same pattern. Smart, capable people were doing serious work inside systems that made it harder than it needed to be to act, coordinate, protect capacity or invest in what the organization would actually need later.

I hold a Bachelor of Science in Biology with an emphasis in pre-medicine and a Master of Public Health from the University of Illinois Springfield.
